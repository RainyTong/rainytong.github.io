About me

My research interests include Wireless Networking, Neural Network Acceleration and Reinforcement Learning. I enjoyed using tools from machine learning to work on real problems.

In the second half of 2019, I was a visiting student researcher at UIUC, where I worked with Professor Haitham Hassanieh. We designed a Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) based MAC protocol for Wireless Network-on-Chips in multicore processors, where each core is augmented with a millimeter wave wireless transceiver. Besides, I also worked on the optimal wireless rate adaptation using thompson sampling.

Prior to that, I mainly worked on the deep convolutional neural network compression and acceleration with Professor Weiping Li at USTC. As the teamleader, we proposed an RL-based pruning architecture to decide which filter should be pruned based on their importance and achieved both higher acceleration ratio and less accuracy loss than the conventional methods.
